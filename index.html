About Us

Lightning Coalition Robotics is a fast-growing, student-driven FTC team based out of The Key School in Annapolis, Maryland. Throughout the season, students learn how to think, design, code, and build like engineers. Each year, Lightning Coalition students teach each other about programming, electronic systems, and practical engineering while buildng a robot to compete in the FTC challenge.

Outreach 2019

We introduced an FLL team, Thundercats 31745, a team based out of Annapolis, Maryland, to the ideas of FTC and how FTC works on February 2nd. We divided into three different groups. One for explaining the notebook, one for explaining the code, and one for explaining the robot. Griffin Dugan (the leader of the notebook) explained the idea of the notebook to the visitors as they are an FLL team they are not used to an engineering notebook. Noah Simon (the leader of the coding) explained how the java code used with FTC differs from the coding system which FLL uses. Gabe Ruoff and Ethan Baker (the leaders of the building) ran a demo of what our robot can do by driving around and picking of blocks. We also let the visitors drive the robot. We explained how we have to drive the robot as well as use autonomous since FLL is solely autonomous.

We also had another outreach event during the Annapolis Book Festival. We hosted an activity for anyone all ages to try out driving our robot. Mainly younger children arrived to try the robot out. They seemed to find it fascinating.
